Guass-Sidel Method:

1. Initialise each Φi,j to some initial guess.
2. Apply the boundary conditions.
3. For every interior grid point, calculate the quantity and replace Φi,j with Φ∗i,j
Φ∗i,j = 1/4 (Φi+1,j + Φi−1,j + Φi,j+1 + Φi,j−1 − σ(xi, yj )δx^2).
5. Repeat from step 2 until the difference between the latest two approximations is
smaller than some set tolerance everywhere.

Grid hierarchy – Build a series of uniform grids: h,2h,4h,…,2^Lh,2h,4h,…,2Lh where h=L/N and N=2L (power‑of‑two makes coarsening trivial).
Pre‑smoothing – Apply ν₁ steps of a red‑black Gauss‑Seidel (or Jacobi) smoother on the fine grid.
Residual computation – r=f−Au.
Restriction – Full‑weighting (inject coarse‑grid residual).
Coarse‑grid solve – Recursively call the V‑cycle on the next coarser level (or do a direct solve on the coarsest grid, e.g., a few Jacobi iterations).
Prolongation – Linear interpolation of the coarse‑grid correction to the fine grid.
Post‑smoothing – Apply ν₂ steps of the same smoother.
Boundary handling – Dirichlet values are kept fixed; they are simply never updated.
